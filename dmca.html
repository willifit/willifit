DMCA Notice & Takedown Policy

Last updated: April 20, 2026

Willifit respects intellectual property rights and complies with the Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA), 17 U.S.C. § 512. If you believe content on willifit.ai infringes your copyright, you may send a formal takedown notice to our designated agent.

What a valid notice must contain

Your notice must include all six of the following elements or it is not legally effective:

  1. A physical or electronic signature of the copyright owner, or a person authorized to act on the owner's behalf
  2. Identification of the copyrighted work claimed to be infringed (or a representative list if multiple works)
  3. Identification of the allegedly infringing material, including its URL on willifit.ai or sufficient detail to locate it
  4. Your contact information: name, address, phone number, and email
  5. A statement of good-faith belief that the use is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law
  6. A statement, under penalty of perjury, that the information in the notice is accurate and that you are authorized to act on behalf of the copyright owner

Designated Agent

Willifit has registered a Designated Agent with the U.S. Copyright Office to receive DMCA takedown notices. The agent's contact information is also published in the Copyright Office's public DMCA Directory.

What happens after we receive a notice

Upon receipt of a complete and valid notice, Willifit will:

  1. Remove or disable access to the allegedly infringing material within a reasonable time, typically within 24 to 48 hours
  2. Notify the user who posted the material (where applicable) and provide them with a copy of the notice
  3. Log the notice for our records as required by law

Counter-notices

If you are a user whose content has been removed and you believe the removal was a mistake or misidentification, you may submit a counter-notice. A valid counter-notice must include:

  1. Your physical or electronic signature
  2. Identification of the removed material and its pre-removal location
  3. A statement, under penalty of perjury, that you have a good-faith belief the material was removed as a result of mistake or misidentification
  4. Your name, address, and phone number, and a statement consenting to the jurisdiction of the federal district court for your address (or Clark County, Nevada, if outside the United States) and agreeing to accept service of process from the original notice sender

Submit counter-notices to dmca@willifit.ai.

Repeat infringers

Willifit will terminate, in appropriate circumstances, the accounts or access of users who are repeat copyright infringers.

Bad-faith notices

Sending a false DMCA notice — claiming infringement of material you do not own, or misrepresenting authorization — is perjury under 17 U.S.C. § 512(f) and may result in liability for damages, including attorney fees. We may forward obviously fraudulent or harassing notices to our counsel and to the party whose content was improperly targeted.
